How to use a three card spread

There's no one way to set out a spread.

People use tarot cards in different ways. Some use them as a spiritual practice; the cards are used to commune with spirits, deities, or just the cosmic energy of the universe. Some take a more secular approach and use the cards as a meditative practice, using the cards to break outside of their assumptions and gain a new persepctive.


You assign the meanings of the position.

What those meanings are really don't matter, as long as you decide on all three before you draw the cards. Below is a list of ideas:


  1. Past, Present, Future: Most often used in spiritual practices, but it's not impossible to do a secular reading. This can be used to get a general snapshot of a person, location, or object.


  2. Option 1, Option 2, Deciding factor: A spread to aid in decision making.


  3. Current skills, Potential, Opportunities: This spread can help with career decisions.


  4. Situation, Obstacle, Advice: If you feel stuck, this spread can help with problem solving.


  5. You, Your partner, Your relationship: Used mostly in compatability readings, but can also help with long-term partners who feel disconnected and don't know how to start the conversation.


  6. Goal, Caution, Advice: This spread helps you focus on your goals, armed with both guidance and warnings.
